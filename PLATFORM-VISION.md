# Platform Vision

## Cutler is the first proof-of-concept

CUTLERLAWNS.COM should remain grounded in the real Cutler Lawns / Cutler Property Preservation & Landscape Services business. Cutler is the first focused implementation, the first vertical proof-of-concept, and the first business instance.

The larger product vision is not merely a landscaping website. The long-term goal is a reusable business-startup and operations platform for local service companies.

## Broader goal

Build a configurable business launcher and operations platform that helps a small service-business owner go from an idea to a working digital foundation.

A future user should be able to describe the business they are starting in plain language, such as:

- “I’m starting a handyman business.”
- “I’m starting a lawn care company.”
- “I’m starting a cleaning company.”
- “I’m starting a pressure washing business.”
- “I’m starting a mobile mechanic service.”
- “I’m starting a painting company.”
- “I’m starting a property maintenance business.”

The platform should then help generate and configure the public presence, private tools, and operating workflows needed to launch and run that business.

## Small businesses need operations, not just websites

Most small business owners do not only need a website. They need a working business system.

The platform should eventually give them:

- a public presence;
- a way to get leads;
- a way to quote work;
- a way to schedule work;
- a way to prove work was done;
- a way to invoice;
- a way to get paid;
- a way to track customers;
- a way to track jobs;
- a way to understand business performance.

This is “business in a box” for local service companies.

## What the platform should generate

Future business instances should be able to generate or configure:

- website;
- brand copy;
- service pages;
- quote forms;
- contact forms;
- customer portal;
- worker or crew portal where applicable;
- admin dashboard;
- estimate templates;
- invoice templates;
- photo proof workflows;
- job tracking workflows;
- product and service catalog;
- basic reporting;
- industry-specific calculators;
- AI-assisted content and visuals;
- launch checklist;
- operating checklist.

## Public website plus private business tools

The platform should combine two layers:

1. Public-facing site and lead-generation tools.
2. Authenticated business operations tools for customers, workers, crews, admins, and owners.

Cutler currently demonstrates this direction through public services, quote/contact flows, AI Yard Builder planning, and documentation for customer, worker, admin, proof, invoicing, and reporting tools.

## AI-assisted configuration

The future experience should feel like business-building by conversation, not traditional coding. A user should be able to use plain language, guided forms, and eventually voice/chat commands such as:

- “Make this a handyman business.”
- “Add drywall repair, TV mounting, light plumbing, and furniture assembly.”
- “Make the tone more professional.”
- “Add emergency service.”
- “Create a quote form for pressure washing.”
- “Add a before/after photo workflow.”
- “Build a customer portal.”
- “Make invoices available online.”
- “Create a maintenance plan option.”

## Cutler must stay focused

Do not overbuild the full SaaS platform yet. For now:

- keep Cutler working as the first focused implementation;
- document the larger direction;
- avoid hardcoding everything so tightly to Cutler that future templates cannot reuse it;
- structure services, catalogs, calculators, workflows, and portals with future reuse in mind where practical.
