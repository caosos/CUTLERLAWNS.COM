# Product Roadmap

This roadmap describes the path from Cutler as the first focused implementation to a reusable business-startup and operations platform.

## Phase 1 — Cutler public site foundation

- Cutler public site.
- Harvested assets.
- Documentation.
- Service pages.
- Gallery/stories.
- Contact and quote flows.
- Commercial-first positioning with residential support.

## Phase 2 — Cutler business platform planning

- Cutler business platform docs.
- Customer portal plan.
- Worker portal plan.
- Admin dashboard plan.
- Security/privacy plan.
- Invoicing/payments plan.
- Job proof workflow plan.

## Phase 3 — Cutler operational MVP

- Jobs.
- Service requests.
- Before/after photos.
- Estimates/invoices scaffold.
- Customer records.
- Property records.
- Basic admin dashboard.
- Basic worker/crew job workflow.

## Phase 4 — AI Yard Builder backend

- Real source-photo upload flow.
- AI Yard Builder backend.
- Saved AI concepts.
- Project cart.
- Estimate conversion from concepts and selected services.
- Server-side AI provider abstraction.

## Phase 5 — Generalize into business templates

- Business template model.
- Template configuration files.
- Reusable vertical system.
- Service/catalog configuration.
- Calculator modules.
- Workflow/checklist templates.
- Public-page sections driven by content/config where practical.

## Phase 6 — Multi-business deployment model

- Multi-business SaaS or self-hosted business instances.
- Business onboarding flow.
- AI business setup wizard.
- Instance-specific branding, services, users, customers, invoices, and settings.
- Clear separation between platform core, vertical template, and business instance data.

## Phase 7 — Voice/chat-based business builder

- “Tell it what business you are starting.”
- Plain-language business configuration.
- Automatic site/tool generation.
- Voice/chat editing of services, tone, forms, calculators, pages, portals, and workflows.
- Guided launch and operating checklists.

## Guardrail

Do not skip the Cutler implementation. The platform should become reusable because Cutler proves the operational model, not because the repo becomes abstract before the first business works.
