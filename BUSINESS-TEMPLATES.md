# Business Templates

## What a business template is

A business template is an industry-specific configuration package for a local service business. It should describe what that type of business usually sells, what workflows it needs, what public pages it should launch with, and what private operations tools it should enable.

Cutler is the first implementation of the landscaping / property-services template.

## What belongs in a template

Each business template should eventually include:

- industry name;
- common services;
- common forms;
- common calculators;
- common job statuses;
- common customer portal needs;
- common worker portal needs;
- common estimate fields;
- common invoice fields;
- common photo proof needs;
- recommended pages;
- recommended calls to action;
- recommended public website sections;
- recommended admin tools;
- launch checklist;
- operating checklist.

## How templates differ by industry

Templates should share platform primitives but differ in industry configuration. For example, every business may need customers, jobs, invoices, payments, and media uploads, but the services, calculators, proof requirements, checklists, and customer questions can vary widely.

A landscaping template may need square-footage calculators, mulch/sod workflows, before/after yard photos, and seasonal cleanup services. A cleaning template may need room counts, recurring checklist completion, move-out cleaning packages, and recurring billing. A handyman template may need punch lists, hourly/project estimates, repair photos, and property maintenance requests.
